## Design Context

### Scope
This design context applies only to the `/rendered` routes and their supporting components, styles, and assets.

The main site experience on `/` and all other non-`/rendered` routes remains the hand-drawn, tldraw-based "work in progress" world. That canvas language is already settled and should not inherit the aesthetic decisions defined below.

### Users
The rendered experience serves four overlapping audiences:

- Hiring managers and recruiters evaluating overall quality and distinctiveness
- Design leads and founders assessing product thinking, craft, and taste
- Peers in design who will notice nuance, originality, and execution quality
- Potential clients looking for confidence, credibility, and a reason to get in touch

Their job is not only to review work samples, but to understand Prerita's point of view as a designer. The experience should create awe first, then sustain curiosity long enough to drive contact.

### Brand Personality
For the rendered pages, the brand should feel:

- Quirky
- Sharp
- Curious

The voice should feel surprising and self-aware, with edge and personality, but still controlled enough to signal maturity and professional judgment.

### Aesthetic Direction
The project has a deliberate two-world structure:

- The hand-drawn tldraw site is the visible "work in progress"
- The `/rendered` routes are the polished "build" output of that same idea

The rendered experience should therefore feel like a compiled version of the sketchbook world: more precise, more editorial, more composed, but still clearly related to the original concept. It should not feel like a separate brand.

Visual direction for `/rendered`:

- Light mode only
- Bold, modern, design-forward, and memorable
- Influenced by contemporary designer portfolios seen on Awwwards and Dribbble, but not reduced to trend mimicry
- Surprising, edgy, and curiosity-inducing rather than safe, corporate, or neutral
- Polished enough to feel intentional, but never so clean that it loses the original quirk

Anti-direction for `/rendered`:

- No dark-mode portfolio aesthetic
- No generic SaaS minimalism
- No polished direction that erases the "WIP becomes build" concept

### Design Principles
1. Preserve the two-world idea. The rendered pages are a reveal and refinement of the hand-drawn site, not a replacement for it.
2. Design for awe before explanation. The first impression should communicate originality and confidence before the visitor reads deeply.
3. Keep the rendered experience light, sharp, and distinctive. Avoid dark-mode polish and default portfolio aesthetics.
4. Make curiosity lead to contact. The visual system should pull visitors deeper into the work and naturally funnel them toward getting in touch.
5. Be quirky with restraint. Unexpected details, composition, and transitions should add character without turning into noise.
